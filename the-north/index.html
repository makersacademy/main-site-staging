Why switch to a career as a developer?

Not satisfied with your job? Want to go to work and love what you do, day in day out? It’s time for a career change, and coding could be exactly what you’re looking for.

At Makers Academy, we’ve helped everybody from stay-at-home parents and recruiters to scientists and lawyers make their first step towards a career they’re passionate about, in jobs with huge growth opportunities.

The technology sector has been growing rapidly for years, and has been largely unaffected by the recession. With demand for developers growing 20% year-on-year, and supply of developers (mainly graduates of Computer Science degrees), flat lining at 1%, the demand for developers is growing rapidly.

According to Tech Nation, Manchester is the largest tech cluster outside London – with nearly 52,000 employees in the sector. There are 10 tech jobs for every 100 residents, which is pretty awesome when you compare London that has only four jobs for every 100. In short there’s a bigger skills gap in the north so it’s the perfect time to learn to code and start your career as a developer. The tech industry in the north needs you!

Our Education Philosophy

Ruby

Ruby is a general-purpose programming language. The brainchild of many other brilliant languages, it's an intuitive route into programming. Ruby is used in web applications through frameworks like Rails and Sinatra.

HTML

HTML is the building material of the web. We'll guide you towards building beautiful, structured webpages and altering content dynamically, both on the server and client-side.

JavaScript

JavaScript is rapidly becoming the biggest programming language in the world. You can use it anywhere: to animate text on a page, to run a high-throughput server, and to enable your coffee machine to work over WiFi.

git

git is the world's most popular way to manage, share, and distribute code. We embed best GitHub practices from the get-go, which perfectly suits our focus on Pair Programming - and our Hiring Partners.
